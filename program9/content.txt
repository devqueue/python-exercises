A nursery rhyme is a traditional poem or song 
for children in Britain and many other countries
There have been several attempts, across the 
world, to revise nursery rhymes (along with 
fairy tales and popular songs). As recently as 
the late 18th century, rhymes like "Little 
Robin Redbreast" were occasionally cleaned up 
for a young audience.[31] In the late 19th 
century the major concern seems to have been 
violence and crime, which led some children's 
publishers in the United States like Jacob 
Abbot and Samuel Goodrich to change Mother 
Goose rhymes
